    Culture 文化drivingdrivingAmericans have long had a ‘love affair’ with the automobile (also car). Almost everybody over the age of 15 is a driver and most households have a vehicle. American life is arranged so that people can do many things from their cars. There are drive-in banks, post offices, restaurants, movie theatres and even some churches.美国人长期以来对汽车（也称为汽车）有着“热爱”。几乎所有 15 岁以上的人都是司机，大多数家庭都拥有车辆。美国人的生活安排得很好，人们可以用汽车做很多事情。这里有汽车银行、邮局、餐馆、电影院，甚至还有一些教堂。In Britain the percentage of the population who are drivers is slightly less but, as in the US, many people prefer to use their car rather than public transport, because it is more convenient and because they like to be independent. In order to reduce pollution, the government tries to encourage people to use other forms of transport by making driving expensive. In particular, it puts a heavy tax on fuel and increases the annual road tax for cars that cause heavy pollution. Congestion charging is used to persuade people to avoid driving their cars in some city centres.在英国，开车的人口比例略低，但与美国一样，许多人更喜欢使用汽车而不是公共交通工具，因为它更方便，而且因为他们喜欢独立。为了减少污染，政府试图通过提高驾驶费用来鼓励人们使用其他形式的交通工具。特别是，它对燃油征收重税，并增加了造成严重污染的汽车的年度道路税。拥堵费用于说服人们避免在一些市中心开车。To some people the make and quality of their car reflects their status in society, and it is important to them to get a smart new car every few years. In Britain since 2001 the registration number of a car shows the place and date of registration but older number plates can be used and a personalized number plate (= a plate that spells out the owner's name or initials) may also suggest status. Many people prefer to buy a small, economical car, or get a second-hand one. Cars in the US are often larger than those in Britain and though fuel is cheaper, insurance is expensive. In the US car license plates, commonly called tags, are given by the states. New ones must be bought when a driver moves to another state. The states use the plates to advertise themselves: Alabama plates say ‘Heart of Dixie’ and have a small heart on them, and Illinois has ‘Land of Lincoln’.对于一些人来说，汽车的品牌和质量反映了他们的社会地位，每隔几年购买一辆智能新车对他们来说很重要。在英国，自 2001 年起，汽车的登记号显示登记地点和日期，但可以使用较旧的车牌，个性化车牌（= 拼出车主姓名或缩写的车牌）也可能表明身份。许多人更喜欢购买一辆小型经济型汽车，或者买一辆二手车。美国的汽车通常比英国的汽车大，尽管燃油便宜，但保险价格昂贵。在美国，汽车牌照（通常称为标签）由各州颁发。当司机搬到另一个州时必须购买新的。各州用这些车牌来宣传自己：阿拉巴马州的车牌上写着“迪克西之心”，上面有一个小心形，伊利诺伊州的车牌上写着“林肯之地”。In Britain, before a person can get a driving licence they must pass an official driving test, which includes a written test on a computer of the Highway Code (= the rules that all road users must obey) and a practical driving exam. Only people aged 17 or over are allowed to drive. Learner drivers who have a provisional licence must display an L-plate, a large red ‘L’, on their car, and be supervised by a qualified driver. The US has no national driver's license (NAmE), but instead licenses are issued by each state. Most require written tests, an eye test and a short practical test. The minimum age for getting a license is normally 16, although some states will issue a learner's permit to drivers as young as 14. Many states now apply a system of graduated licenses in which young drivers are first required to have an intermediate license for a period of time before being given a full license. An intermediate license may, for example, prevent driving alone at particular times of the day or require the driver to take special classes if they drive badly. Americans have to get a new driver's license if they move to another state. In both Britain and the US drivers need a special licence to drive certain types of vehicle, for example a bus, lorry or motorcycle. 在英国，在获得驾驶执照之前，他们必须通过官方驾驶考试，其中包括《公路法规》（=所有道路使用者必须遵守的规则）的电脑笔试和实际驾驶考试。仅允许年满 17 岁或以上的人驾驶。拥有临时驾照的学习驾驶员必须在车上展示 L 牌照（一个大的红色“L”），并由合格驾驶员监督。美国没有国家驾驶执照 (NAmE)，而是由每个州颁发执照。大多数都需要笔试、视力测试和简短的实践测试。获得驾照的最低年龄通常为 16 岁，但有些州会向年仅 14 岁的驾驶员颁发学习驾照。许多州现在采用分级驾照制度，其中年轻驾驶员首先需要持有一段时间的中级驾照获得正式执照之前的时间。例如，中级驾照可以防止在一天中的特定时间单独驾驶，或者要求驾驶员在驾驶不好的情况下参加特殊课程。美国人如果搬到另一个州就必须获得新的驾驶执照。在英国和美国，驾驶员需要特殊许可证才能驾驶某些类型的车辆，例如公共汽车、卡车或摩托车。In Britain people drive on the left and in the US they drive on the right. Generally British and US drivers are relatively careful and polite but there is dangerous driving. In the US many of the deaths due to traffic accidents are caused by drivers who have drunk alcohol. Drink-driving (NAmE drunk driving, driving under the influence or driving while intoxicated) (= driving a car after drinking alcohol) is also a serious problem in Britain. On many British roads speed cameras have been set up to catch drivers who go too fast. In the US the main job of state highway patrols is to prevent speeding.在英国，人们靠左行驶，而在美国，人们靠右行驶。一般来说，英国和美国的司机都比较小心和礼貌，但也存在危险驾驶的情况。在美国，许多交通事故造成的死亡是由饮酒司机造成的。酒后驾驶（NAmE 醉酒驾驶、酒后驾驶或醉酒驾驶）（= 饮酒后驾驶汽车）在英国也是一个严重的问题。英国许多道路上都安装了超速摄像头来捕捉超速行驶的司机。在美国，州公路巡逻的主要工作是防止超速。Many drivers pay a monthly fee to their insurance company or to an automobile association, who will arrange for someone to come and help them if their car breaks down or if they are involved in an accident. In Britain the main ones are the AA (Automobile Association) and the RAC (Royal Automobile Club), and in the US the largest is the American Automobile Association (AAA).许多司机每月向保险公司或汽车协会支付费用，如果他们的汽车发生故障或发生事故，他们会安排有人来帮助他们。英国主要是AA（汽车协会）和RAC（皇家汽车俱乐部），美国最大的是美国汽车协会（AAA）。

Vocabulary.com Dictionary car
car
(once / 55 pages)
n
Your car is your automobile, your wheels, your vehicle. A car can also be another kind of moving compartment — such as a railroad car, a cable car, or a trolley car.
The word car has been around much longer than the automobile. It comes from the Latin carrus, a kind of wagon the Romans discovered from the Gauls, a European people the Romans conquered. It shows up in Italian and Spanish (carro, meaning "a car or a cart," and it also survives in various forms in modern Irish, Gaelic, Welsh, Breton, German, and other languages. When you’re listening to your engine purr, you can thank the Gauls. And the Romans.
